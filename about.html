About Us


Gonzaga is a municipality situated on the northern coast of Cagayan, in the Philippines. Known for its scenic coastline and lush landscapes, Gonzaga offers a blend of natural beauty and rich history. It is a serene destination that appeals to tourists seeking an escape from the hustle of urban life, offering pristine beaches, historic sites, and cultural experiences.



Historical Background

Originally known as Wangag, Gonzaga was renamed in honor of Don Gracio Gonzaga, the first governor of Cagayan. The town has a history dating back to the 18th century, with early inhabitants including the Negritos and Ilokano settlers. Missionaries played a significant role in the development of the area, attracting more settlers and merging two key settlements: Wangag and Gampao (meaning “mountainous”). The unified settlement was eventually called Rumag-ay, symbolizing progress.

It officially became a municipality in 1918, following a determined push led by Francisco Torres, a notable leader who rallied the Negrito laborers to help secure its recognition. Gonzaga’s name is tied to the memory of Don Gracio Gonzaga, highlighting the town's historical dedication to preserving the legacy of its leaders and pioneers. The community’s spirit of resilience and unity is a testament to its vibrant heritage and growth over the years.

Culture and Heritage

Gonzaga's culture is deeply rooted in its historical mix of indigenous and Ilokano influences. The town celebrates local traditions through various festivals, religious practices, and culinary delights. The patroness of Gonzaga, Nuestra Señora del Pilar, has a special place in the community's heart, symbolizing the strong religious heritage of the town.

Events and Festival

Gonzaga celebrates local and regional festivals that showcase traditional music, dances, and foods, fostering a sense of community and pride. Visitors can experience authentic cultural performances, parades, and culinary feasts that highlight the town's unique offerings.
